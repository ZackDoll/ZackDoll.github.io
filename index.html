About Me

I'm a CS student at UCLA who gets hooked by making things that make me feel like Tony Stark. Most recently, I built an ASL translator that combines computer vision with LSTMs to recognize sign language in real-time from webcam footage. I merged two separate datasets to boost accuracy, and watching it actually work for the first time was pretty surreal.

My GitHub is basically a graveyard of random ideas I got excited about. I like trying new things and picking up whatever seems interesting at the time, even if it's not particularly useful. Python's my main language, but I've also built full-stack web apps with React and Node.js, like my MLB pitch predictor that analyzes 12+ million pitches to forecast what's coming next.

When I'm not working on course projects or debugging something at 2am, I'm probably trying to figure out why my code worked yesterday but doesn't work today, or convincing myself that "just one more feature" won't take that long.
